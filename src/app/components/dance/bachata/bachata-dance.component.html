Bachata
Bachata is a dance form which originated in the Dominican Republic in the second half of the 20th century. It has its roots in Bolero and is traditionally danced in a square.
Its expansion to the Western civilisation brought influences from Salsa and Ballroom dances, and, consequently, the creation of Modern Bachata, which is danced side to side and involves turning patterns and dips.
Eventually, influences from Brazilian Zouk led to the creation of a widely popular dance style, Bachata Sensual, which includes body waves, body isolations and dips.
